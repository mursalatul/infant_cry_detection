Baby Comfort Guide

Recognizing and addressing your baby's needs

Discomfort Signs

Difficulty in sleeping and settling down

Attention: Is your baby crying indicating colic?

Difficulty in feeding

Attention: When feeding becomes stressful or avoided

Intense crying

Attention: Excessive crying may signal pain or distress

Lack of energy

Attention: Baby is less responsive or inactive

Pained facial expressions

Attention: Persistent grimacing or clenched jaw

Tense or stiff body movements

Attention: Arching back or rigid limbs repeatedly

Belly Pain

Crying more than usual

Reason: Gas

Treatment: Bicycle baby's legs

See a doctor if: Fever of 100.4°F (38°C) or above

Spitting up or vomiting

Reason: Constipation

Treatment: Take a look at their diet

See a doctor if: Diarrhea that lasts more than 48 hours

Refusing to eat

Reason: Formula ingredients

Treatment: Take a look at your diet

See a doctor if: Constipation that lasts more than a few days

Unusual sleep disruptions

Reason: Allergies or sensitivities

Treatment: Address feeding issues

See a doctor if: Signs of extreme stress like nonstop screaming

Diarrhea or constipation

Reason: GERD (reflux)

Treatment: Consider a different formula

See a doctor if: Extreme fatigue
